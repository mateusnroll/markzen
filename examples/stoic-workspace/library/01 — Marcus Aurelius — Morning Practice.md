# Morning Practice

> “From my grandfather Verus I learned good morals and the government of my temper.”

*Marcus Aurelius, Thoughts, Book I, George Long translation*

## A page before the day begins

Marcus opens with gratitude for examples received from other people. A practical reading is to make character concrete: name the person, name the quality, then name one action that would embody it today.

### Three columns of attention

1. **Example:** Who has modeled steadiness for me?
2. **Practice:** What can I rehearse today?
3. **Review:** What will count as evidence tonight?

- Recall one observable action, not a reputation.
- Keep the instruction small enough to remember.
- Write it as a verb: *listen*, *pause*, *finish*, or *return*.

#### A compact intention

Today I will meet interruption with **patience**, not ~~perfect calm~~ a quick return to calm. My reminder is `pause → judge → act`.
